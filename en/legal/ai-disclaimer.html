1. Introduction & Critical Warning

⚠️ CRITICAL WARNING - MUST READ BEFORE USING AUTOCOGNITIX

AutoCognitix is an artificial intelligence (AI) system that provides informational vehicle diagnostic guidance only. It is NOT a replacement for professional automotive services, and its recommendations should NEVER be relied upon as the sole basis for vehicle repair decisions.

The AutoCognitix AI system has significant limitations and cannot perform physical vehicle inspections, electrical testing, or professional diagnostics. Vehicle safety systems (brakes, steering, airbags, fuel, electrical) MUST be evaluated by certified professional mechanics.

AutoCognitix is powered by large language models (LLMs) and machine learning algorithms trained on public vehicle diagnostic data, NHTSA recall information, and automotive technical documentation. While our AI system is designed to assist vehicle owners in understanding potential issues, it has inherent limitations and operational constraints that you must understand before using the Service.

This AI Disclaimer explains these limitations, your legal rights, and our liability boundaries. By using AutoCognitix, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to all terms in this disclaimer.

2. AI System Limitations

2.1 What AutoCognitix AI Cannot Do

The AutoCognitix artificial intelligence system has the following technical and operational limitations:

Limitation Description Impact
No Physical Inspection AI cannot physically examine your vehicle, perform visual inspections, or assess external/internal conditions Hidden damage, corrosion, worn components may not be detected
No Electrical Testing AI cannot measure voltage, current, resistance, or test electrical circuits Electrical faults may be misdiagnosed or missed entirely
No Fluid Analysis AI cannot test oil viscosity, coolant pH, transmission fluid condition, or brake fluid Fluid degradation or contamination issues cannot be identified
No Real-Time Scanning AI cannot communicate directly with vehicle electronic control modules (ECMs) or access live data streams Intermittent faults or active sensor failures may not be captured
No Emissions Testing AI cannot perform emissions testing or measure exhaust gas composition Environmental compliance status cannot be verified
Training Data Limitations AI training data has a knowledge cutoff date and may not reflect recent design changes, recalls, or service bulletins Newer vehicle issues may not be recognized; outdated repair procedures may be recommended
Model-Specific Uncertainty AI may confuse similar vehicle models or years with different diagnostic procedures Recommendations appropriate for one model may be incorrect for another
No Safety Assessment AI cannot determine whether a vehicle is safe to operate or when professional service is urgently required You may make dangerous decisions to drive an unsafe vehicle

2.2 AI Hallucination & Uncertainty

Large language models, including AutoCognitix's AI system, are known to occasionally "hallucinate"—that is, generate plausible-sounding but factually incorrect information with high confidence. This occurs because the AI predicts text statistically rather than retrieving information from a verified database.

Potential consequences of AI hallucination include:

  • Incorrect part numbers or specifications for your vehicle
  • Non-existent service procedures or technical bulletins
  • Misidentification of symptoms (attributing brake issues to transmission failure, for example)
  • Estimated repair costs that are significantly higher or lower than actual market rates
  • Recommendations for repairs that your vehicle does not require

2.3 Variability & Inconsistency

The same diagnostic input may produce different results on different occasions, as the AI model includes inherent randomness in text generation. This means:

  • Running the same diagnostic twice may produce slightly different recommendations
  • You should not expect identical output across multiple uses
  • Consistency should not be interpreted as accuracy
  • You cannot rely on AutoCognitix for reproducible results

3. AI Regulation & Compliance

3.1 EU AI Act Compliance

AutoCognitix is classified as a "high-risk" AI system under the EU AI Act 2024 (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689) because it provides recommendations that could affect vehicle safety and user health. We comply with the following requirements:

  • Transparency: Users are informed through this disclaimer that they are interacting with an AI system
  • Human Oversight: Users can request human review of AI recommendations by contacting support@autocognitix.hu
  • Data Governance: Training data is documented and periodically audited for bias
  • Risk Management: Regular testing is performed to identify and mitigate safety risks
  • Documentation: Complete technical documentation of the AI system is maintained and provided to regulatory authorities upon request
  • Incident Reporting: Serious safety incidents are reported to the relevant data protection authority

3.2 GDPR Compliance for AI Processing

Your vehicle data is processed by the AutoCognitix AI system under Article 6(1)(b) GDPR (contract performance) and Article 9(4) GDPR (legitimate interest in providing the Service). You have the right to:

  • Know that AI is processing your data (transparency requirement)
  • Request human review of AI decisions affecting you (Article 22 GDPR)
  • Obtain a meaningful explanation of how AI reached its recommendations (interpretability requirement)
  • Request deletion of data used to train or fine-tune the AI model

3.3 Automated Decision-Making

AutoCognitix recommendations are made through automated decision-making (AI) without human review. However, this is not a "legal or similarly significant effect" (Article 22 GDPR) because:

  • AutoCognitix provides advisory information only; it cannot bind you to any contract or obligation
  • You remain free to disregard AI recommendations entirely
  • Final repair decisions are made solely by you and certified mechanics
  • AutoCognitix does not implement any automated decisions

Nonetheless, you have the right to request human review of any AI recommendation by contacting our Data Protection Officer at dpo@autocognitix.hu.

5. Safety-Critical Warnings

5.1 Safety Systems Requiring Immediate Professional Service

🚨 SAFETY-CRITICAL SYSTEMS - DO NOT DELAY PROFESSIONAL SERVICE 🚨

The following vehicle systems are responsible for preventing accidents, injuries, and fatalities. If ANY of these systems are suspected to be faulty, your vehicle MUST be professionally inspected and repaired immediately before operation.

5.1.1 Brake System

Symptoms Requiring Immediate Professional Service:

  • Brake pedal feels soft, spongy, or goes to the floor
  • Brake warning light illuminates (red circle or square symbol)
  • Visible brake fluid leaks under the vehicle
  • Grinding or metal-on-metal sounds when braking
  • Vehicle pulls significantly to one side when braking
  • Brake pedal vibrates or chatters during braking

Danger: Brake failure can result in complete loss of braking ability and catastrophic accidents. Do not operate the vehicle.

5.1.2 Steering System

Symptoms Requiring Immediate Professional Service:

  • Steering wheel becomes unusually stiff or difficult to turn
  • Steering wheel is loose or feels disconnected from wheels
  • Power steering warning light illuminates
  • Steering fluid leaks visible under vehicle
  • Vehicle drifts or pulls sharply to one side while driving straight
  • Steering wheel does not return to center after turning

Danger: Steering failure means you cannot control vehicle direction. Do not operate the vehicle.

5.1.3 Airbag System

Symptoms Requiring Immediate Professional Service:

  • Airbag warning light (yellow or orange balloon symbol) illuminates
  • Airbag system warning messages appear on dashboard
  • Physical damage to steering wheel, dashboard, or side panels where airbags are housed
  • Wet or corroded-looking airbag module covers

Danger: Airbags may deploy unexpectedly or fail to deploy in a collision. Vehicle occupants are at increased risk of fatal injury. Do not operate the vehicle.

5.1.4 Fuel System

Symptoms Requiring Immediate Professional Service:

  • Strong gasoline smell inside or under the vehicle
  • Visible fuel leaks or wet spots near fuel tank or fill area
  • Engine hesitation, stalling, or difficulty starting
  • Check engine light illuminates with fuel system trouble codes
  • Smoke visible from under the vehicle
  • Fuel odor that worsens with engine operation

Danger: Fuel leaks create fire and explosion hazards. Vehicle may catch fire during operation. Do not operate the vehicle.

5.1.5 Electrical System

Symptoms Requiring Immediate Professional Service:

  • Complete loss of electrical power (lights, instrumentation, starting system fail)
  • Burning smell from electrical components or under the hood
  • Excessive battery drain (dead battery within days of charging)
  • Alternator warning light illuminates
  • Visible sparks or arcing near battery terminals or alternator
  • Multiple warning lights illuminate simultaneously (indicating system failure)

Danger: Complete electrical failure means loss of power steering, power brakes, lights, and starting ability. Vehicle cannot be safely operated or started.

5.2 Do Not Ignore Warning Lights

The AutoCognitix AI system CANNOT tell you definitively whether a warning light indicates a safety-critical problem. When any warning light appears on your dashboard, you should:

  1. Immediately: Get a professional diagnostic scan of the vehicle
  2. Never: Assume a warning light is not serious because you "feel nothing wrong"
  3. Never: Drive extended distances with warning lights illuminated
  4. Never: Ignore brake, steering, or airbag warning lights under any circumstances
