3.1 Safety-Critical Systems

The following vehicle systems are SAFETY-CRITICAL and require immediate attention from a certified professional mechanic if issues are detected or suspected:

  • Brake System: Any issues with brakes, anti-lock braking system (ABS), or brake fluid must be professionally diagnosed and repaired immediately. Brake failures can result in loss of vehicle control and catastrophic accidents.
  • Steering System: Problems with steering, power steering, or steering linkage must be professionally addressed immediately. Steering failure can result in inability to control vehicle direction.
  • Airbag System: Any airbag malfunction must be addressed by a certified professional before operating the vehicle. Airbag failures may prevent critical safety protection in collisions.
  • Fuel System: Fuel line leaks, fuel pump failures, or fuel management issues pose fire and explosion hazards. These MUST be professionally diagnosed and repaired immediately.
  • Electrical System: Battery, alternator, and electrical failures must be professionally evaluated, particularly in cold weather conditions or when affecting critical safety systems.

DO NOT DELAY - PROFESSIONAL SERVICE REQUIRED

If you receive diagnostic guidance relating to any of the above systems, DO NOT operate the vehicle until it has been professionally inspected and repaired by a certified mechanic. Ignoring safety-critical warnings may result in personal injury or death.
