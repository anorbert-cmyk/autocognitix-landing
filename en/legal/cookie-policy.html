1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile phone) when you visit a website. They allow websites to recognize your device, remember your preferences, and enhance your browsing experience. Cookies are widely used across the internet and are considered standard practice in web technology.

Each cookie typically contains:

  • A unique identifier
  • Expiration date (some expire when the browser closes, others persist for weeks or months)
  • Domain information (which websites can access it)
  • Data values specific to your interaction with that website

Cookies do NOT contain malware, viruses, or executable code. They are purely informational text files used to improve your experience.

2. Why We Use Cookies

AutoCognitix uses cookies to:

  • Authenticate Users: Keep you logged in across sessions
  • Remember Preferences: Store your language choice, theme settings, and diagnostic history
  • Improve Performance: Analyze how users interact with our platform
  • Enhance Security: Detect and prevent fraudulent access attempts
  • Provide Analytics: Understand which features are most used and identify areas for improvement
  • Personalize Content: Deliver relevant diagnostic information based on your vehicle history
  • Measure Marketing Effectiveness: Track which advertising channels drive traffic to AutoCognitix

5. Cookie Consent & Management

5.1 Cookie Consent Banner

When you first visit AutoCognitix, a consent banner appears at the bottom of the screen. No analytics or marketing scripts load until you make a choice. The banner offers three options:

  • Accept all: Enables Strictly Necessary + Analytics + Marketing cookies.
  • Reject all: Only Strictly Necessary cookies are active. No tracking scripts load.
  • Manage preferences: Opens a panel with three labelled toggles — one per category. Each toggle defaults to off. You choose what to enable.

Your choice is stored in your browser's localStorage under the key ac_cookie_consent as a JSON record containing your category selections, a timestamp, and a schema version number. No consent tracking cookie is itself placed on your device without necessity. Consent expires after 6 months; the banner reappears automatically to renew your choice.

5.2 Consent Requirements Under GDPR & ePrivacy Directive

Under EU regulations (GDPR Article 7, ePrivacy Directive Article 5(3)), we must obtain your explicit consent before setting non-essential cookies. Our banner is designed so that consent is:

  • Freely given: Rejecting all cookies does not block access to the site.
  • Informed: Each category is described before you consent.
  • Specific: Analytics and Marketing are separate toggles with separate explanations.
  • Unambiguous: Requires an affirmative click. Closing the banner tab or scrolling does not count as consent.
  • Easy to withdraw: The "Cookie preferences" link in the footer reopens the preference panel at any time.

No Pre-Checked Consent

AutoCognitix does NOT use pre-ticked boxes in the consent panel. All optional toggles default to off. Silence or inaction does NOT constitute consent (EDPB Guidelines 05/2020, §3.2.1).

5.3 Managing Your Cookies

You can update your cookie preferences at any time by:

  • Clicking the "Cookie preferences" link in the footer of any page. This reopens the preferences panel immediately.
  • Clearing localStorage in your browser developer tools (key: ac_cookie_consent). The banner will reappear on your next visit.
  • Contacting privacy@autocognitix.hu

6. How to Delete or Disable Cookies

6.1 Browser Settings

You can disable cookies in your browser settings. Instructions for major browsers:

Google Chrome

  1. Click the menu icon (three dots) in the top-right corner
  2. Select Settings → Privacy and Security → Cookies and other site data
  3. Choose "Block all cookies" or manage exceptions for specific sites

Mozilla Firefox

  1. Click the menu icon (three horizontal lines) in the top-right corner
  2. Select Settings → Privacy & Security
  3. Under "Cookies and Site Data," select your preferred option (Allow, Limit to Current Session, or Block)

Apple Safari

  1. Click Safari in the menu bar
  2. Select Preferences → Privacy
  3. Under "Cookies and website data," select your preference

Microsoft Edge

  1. Click the menu icon (three dots) in the top-right corner
  2. Select Settings → Privacy, search, and services
  3. Under "Cookies and other site data," adjust your settings

6.2 Disabling Third-Party Cookies

Most browsers allow you to disable third-party cookies specifically while keeping essential cookies enabled:

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and Security → Third-party cookies → Select "Block third-party cookies"
  • Firefox: Settings → Privacy & Security → Enhanced Tracking Protection → Select "Strict"
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Uncheck "Allow cross-site tracking"

6.3 Opt-Out Services

Some advertisers offer opt-out mechanisms:

  • Google Analytics: Google Analytics Opt-Out Browser Extension
  • Meta (Facebook): Facebook Ads Preferences
  • Network Advertising Initiative: NAI Consumer Opt-Out
  • Digital Advertising Alliance (DAA): DAA Consumer Choice

Impact of Disabling Cookies

If you disable essential cookies, AutoCognitix may not function properly. You may lose:

  • Ability to log in to your account
  • Saved diagnostic history
  • Personalized recommendations
  • Language and theme preferences

We recommend allowing at least essential and functional cookies for the best experience.

7. Your Rights Under GDPR

7.1 Right to Access & Transparency

You have the right to know:

  • What cookies are being set on your device
  • Which organizations are setting them (first-party vs. third-party)
  • How long they persist
  • What data they contain
  • How they are used to process your personal data

7.2 Right to Consent & Withdrawal

You have the right to:

  • Give or withhold consent for non-essential cookies
  • Change your mind and withdraw consent at any time
  • Consent to some categories of cookies while rejecting others
  • Not have your choices treated as a condition of using the Service

7.3 Right to Erasure (Deletion)

You can request that AutoCognitix delete cookies associated with your account. Contact privacy@autocognitix.hu with the request "Delete all cookies associated with my account [email]." We will comply within 30 days unless the cookie is necessary for security.

7.4 Right to Data Portability

You have the right to request a copy of all data processed through cookies, in a machine-readable format. Submit requests to privacy@autocognitix.hu.

7.5 Cookie Policy Complaints

If you believe AutoCognitix is not complying with GDPR or ePrivacy Directive regarding cookies, you may lodge a complaint with the National Authority for Data Protection and Freedom of Information (NAIH).
